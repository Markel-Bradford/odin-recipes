Chimichurri Sauce

Description

Chimichurri sauce is a condiment that originated in Argenetina and Uruguay and is typically served with grilled meats and veggies. It can be used as a marinade or a suce topping due to its ingredients to elevate your dish. In America, Chimichurri is commonly paired with grilled steaks to make a delicious and irresitable pairing.

Making Chimichurri is a faily simple process. The ingredients it calls for are a few spices, some fresh aromatics, and some oil and vinegar. Once you have obtained all of the ingredients listed below, the next step is mixing them together at the correct ratio. Try the recipe below and let us know what you think!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • ¾ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 ½ teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 ½ teaspoons pepper sauce (such as Frank's Red Hot®)
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Directions

  1. Combine parsley, oil, vinegar, hot sauce, oregano, cumin, garlic, and salt in a blender or food processor.
  2. Mix on medium speed until ingredients are blended, about 10 seconds.
  3. Enjoy!
